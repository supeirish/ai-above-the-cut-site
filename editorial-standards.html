Editorial Standards

How AI Above the Cut is sourced, verified, and corrected.

AI Above the Cut exists to give business leaders signal over noise. That promise only holds if the method behind it is visible. This is the standing explanation of how the brief is produced.

What we cover

The AI developments that matter for business leaders — and what to do next. That means primary evidence, product and pricing moves, regulatory action, enterprise behavior, and healthcare developments, read through an executive lens. We don't curate celebrity. We curate evidence, interpretation, implementation, and disagreement.

How sources are selected

We read a fixed spine of the field's highest-signal voices, organized in tiers: Core weekly voices we read every week; rotating specialists we monitor and surface when they publish primary work (they do not write for or participate in AI Above the Cut — we monitor and cite them); frontier principals whose releases set the agenda; and primary institutional feeds — regulators, standards bodies, primary research, and official lab documentation. The full roster lives on the About page.

How claims are verified

Strong claims are checked against a different source category than the one that made them — a vendor claim is tested against a researcher, operator, regulator, or independent analysis, not accepted at face value. We label what we're showing you:

  • Verified fact — corroborated by a primary or independent source.
  • Vendor claim — asserted by a company about its own product; treated as a claim until confirmed.
  • Reported but unconfirmed — surfaced because it matters, flagged because it isn't yet verified.
  • Our interpretation — the editor's read, marked as judgment, not fact.
  • Our counter-case — the strongest argument against our own read.

Primary-source preference

Where a paper, filing, system card, or official document exists, we link to it — not to the coverage of it. When we cite secondary reporting, we say so.

How AI tools are used

AI tools assist with monitoring, organization, and drafting. Sources, claims, interpretations, and final publication decisions are reviewed by the editor. AI does not decide what is true or what we publish.

Corrections

When we get something wrong, we correct it — visibly, on the original issue as well as the current one, with a dated note. See the Corrections log for the full history. Corrections are a feature of a signal-first publication, not something to bury.

Independence and conflicts

AI Above the Cut is independent. It is not sponsored, and it represents no employer or vendor. If that ever changes — a sponsorship, an affiliation, a position in a company we cover — we will disclose it plainly and in context.
